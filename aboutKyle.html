  • Hello! Welcome to my website!
    I graduated in 2019 from Purdue University with Bachelors in Mechanical Engineering. I have three years of experience working at Newport News Shipbuilding (NNS) where I have carried the title of Systems Test Engineer, Structural Engineer, Software Test Engineer, Mechanical Engineer, and Model Based Engineer. I had the opportunity to participate in the PEER Program at NNS, which allowed me to transfer to up to four different departments in the span of two years. Each Position after Systems Test Engineer was held for 6 months, until I landed at my permanent role as a Model Based Engineer.
  • Newport News Shipbuilding: Test Engineer
    I worked as Test Engineer for 1.5 years on Los Angeles class submarines, namely the USS Columbus, Helena & Boise. I assisted the crew in completion and verification of restoration of said submarines. I ensured that all piping and electrical components of various systems could handle the stress and strain of all sea and combat situations.
  • Newport News Shipbuilding: Structrual Engineer
    As a structural engineer, I worked upon the Navy's newest class of submarine, Columbia. My main project created a structurally sound platform to ensure a 600 ton section of the submarine could be safely stored and operated on for building. I also worked on various structures for various component transfers, such as loading of pumps and valves.
  • Newport News Shipbuilding: Software Test Engineer
    As a Software Test Engineer, I was responsible for testing of in house software that would control most valves, safety systems, and electrical components on board the Ford Class Aircraft Carriers. My primary responsibilities included developing test procedures to ensure that changes made to code and systems were functioning properly within the team. Once testing began, we would test each other's procedures, and provide any solutions for bugs that were found.
  • Newport News Shipbuilding: Mechanical Engineer
    I then moved on to be a Mechanical Engineer for our model based manufacturing department. As a Mechanical Engineer, I was a checker for 3D drawings of piping and ventilation for the aircraft carrier Enterprise. I interfaced with developers of 3D drawings to verify all dimensions, callouts, and all necessary information for the trades to construct the aircraft carrier.
  • Newport News Shipbuilding: Model Based Engineer
    My current job as a Systems Engineer has me upkeeping and maintaining the system database for two different classes of submarines. My team is responsible for troubleshooting and solving any known issues within the database in how it receives information. We will test any bug fixes, and move the program forward to allow for the value stream members to more easily and efficiently do there jobs.

  • Simple EQ (C++)
    3 Band EQ that cut lows, highs, and a peak filter to boost or cut mids. Change filter slope on 4 different settings for lows and highs. Turn off and on any band. See the filter band in real time at the top of the window.

    Source Code can be found here: https://github.com/KiTiKDub/SimpleEQ
  • 3-band Compressor (C++)
    Multiband compressor with bands for low, mids, and highs. Each band has adjustable crossover frequencies to dial in exactly where you need your compression.

    Each band has adjustable attack, release, threshold, and ratio, with the ability to mute, solo, and bypass the band.

    Visualize your compression with a FFT spectrum analyzer to see where you may need to dial down your sound. Each band has the threshold mapped to the analyzer so you can see exactly when your compression will start. The analyzer will also show the response of the compression so you can see exactly how it is responding to your input.

    Source code can be found here https://github.com/KiTiKDub/MBComp
